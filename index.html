HUES Platform


What is the HUES Platform?

The Holistic Urban Energy Simulation (HUES) Platform is an open source platform to facilitate research on the simulation and optimization of distributed energy systems. Resources in the HUES Platform include:

1. Models

The Model repository includes a number of models for simulating and optimizing building- and district-scale energy systems. The code for these models is readily accessible, and may be downloaded and extended upon freely. Access the model repository >>>

2. Modeling tools

The Energy Hub Optimizer is an open source tool for optimizing the design and operation of distributed multi-energy systems at the level of buildings and districts. The Energy Hub Optimizer is developed in Matlab and the Aimms. Access the Energy Hub Optimizer >>>

3. Data

The Technology database includes data on the technical and economic properties of distributed technologies for energy conversion and storage. Access to the data in the technology database is unrestricted. Access the technology database >>>

The Swiss energy supply and demand web service includes aggregated yearly heat demand and solar rooftop PV potential in Switzerland, accessible at either the commune or pixel level. Access to the web service is restricted to participants in the SCCER FEEB&D project. Access the Swiss energy demand and supply web service >>>

4. Code

The HUES platform includes a number of code snippets and scripts which may be useful to researchers dealing with the simulation and optimization of distributed energy systems. Access the code repository >>>



Development of the HUES Platform is financially supported by the projects SCCER FEEB&D and CCEM SECURE.
